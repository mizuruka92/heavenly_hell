Unknown to many, Asgard was actually just a small region of a larger worldly sphere, with a size so big that most eyes could not fathom. The sphere itself circles around the sun just like how midgard and other realms would do.

To the naked eye, the sphere would appear as a gaseous giant, yet within it the Creator had woven His magic — shaping realms unseen by mortals, the final destination men would call heaven.
There, gods and goddesses dwelled in their own dominions, and Asgard was one of them.

I was actually born in another worldly sphere — the largest of all — made of the same gas and crowned with magical lands where giants resided. Everyone called that realm Jotunheim. Then one day Lord Odin invited my brothers and me to live in Asgard, or rather, to be held captive, for we had no freedom.

My eldest brother, with his mighty jaws, was bound deep within Asgard. Though we were close I could never visit him, I could only hear his growl.
My second brother was cast into the seas of Midgard, his body growing so vast that only the endless ocean could contain it.
As for me — I slept in a guest chamber. No net ensnared me, yet everyone shunned me. They knew amidst being beautiful, my lower part was nothing but a decayed moving corpse. Everyone would define me as a monster, and no one would fathom a monster with beautiful face, so I chose to hide.

Other than that, us the 3 siblings had an astronomical hearings and sights. Our eyes could see far to the edges of Asgard, even farther, and our ears could hear even the worms biting away leaves at the edge of Asgard. However, I was the only one with the curse or maybe a blessing, that my memory was far stronger than anything, I could remember every second of the past as if it just happened the other day. I could retell stories as long I see or hear it, and eventually my astronomical senses added more layers to what I remembered.

Sometimes, I would sit next to the window, but far enough from the opening so no one could see that I was actually there. Through my hearings and sights, I could hear the Einherjars training far away.

For me the Einherjars were weird. They fought daily. They died daily. Each coming night, they would resurect and rose anew. Those who survived would spend the night in splendor — feasting on the finest food in all of Asgard, served by none other than Lady Freya, the first Valkyrie. Those who fell would still enjoy the feast, but mocked regularly for being so weak to die, as a badge of dishonor.

Among them, Johann was the strongest, the fiercest — Lady Freya’s favorite. She regarded him almost like a son. Yet for all his might, kindness was not among his virtues. If I were to write fantasy, I would tell that he traded compassion for power, though in reality he was just strong without heart. Arrogant, rude, and overconfident would be an understatement of his deeds. And on that day, he slew countless of his fellow Einherjar — all for the pleasure of the honor during the feast of the night.
