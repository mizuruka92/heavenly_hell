It was long past bedtime for the Asgardians. The cold of the night could pierce one's bones	— but a deed that was colder than the night had happened. Uneasiness filled me. I stood and listened, far from where the Halls of Valhalla should stand.

I rarely went out unless summoned, or when something truly important arose — though I always justified myself because nothing was as important as hiding under the blanket with the door shut.

All this time, I heard weeping. I heard whispers. I heard people badmouthing me. I heard my brother growling, longing to be freed. Yet over and over, I never once took a step outside.

I knew it was none of my business, but what I heard was not a simple matter. It was not curiosity, it was something deeper than that. A cry. A woman. A man’s laughter full of joy in ecstatic pleasure. Only a liar would dismiss the thought that something wicked had happened; only a fool would say they could not connect the dots. Or maybe, it was plainly understood by me who could hear.

I stopped, hand on the doorknob. I gathered my courage — and failed — until I realized that if I hesitated any longer, I would move beyond just regret. So I went out, silent but swift, toward the Halls of Valhalla.
