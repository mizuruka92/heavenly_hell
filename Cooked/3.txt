Midgard had longer day and night compared to Asgard. The sun rose and dawned upon us before Midgard experienced even half of its day. However, as the sun was far and dim from our realm, we attuned our understanding of day and night by the light produced by the mana stream itself, hence our day and night had the same duration as it was in Midgard.

All of these were only possible because of the power of the Creator. No one had ever met the Creator, yet His ancient magic shaped the world — the universe itself. Even we could see the larger sea of stars far across the sky, like a great pinwheel turning in the void, and we knew it was all His creation as well. Even a Goddess like me wondered if there was life beyond it.

As night came, I continued my silent nights reading the books my father brought me before I slept. Again, the books were also the saviour of my senses as the nightly feast was always very loud even though Valhalla was quite a distance away from Valaskjalf. I could hear every whisper, every laugh, every word, every song they sang, every delay between breaths. I could hear and listen every conversation. It was tedious at first, but over the course of millenia, I could already get used to hearing everything at once, and also to remember everything without missing any detail.

The book I read that night was quite boring, yet once I began, I had to finish. However, it became its own challenge as my eyes grew heavy due to boredom and my will faltered. Soon, I fell asleep — though, like all others, one only knows they have slept once they were awakened.

And when I woke, it was to the sound of weeping — a deep wound made audible — and I learned that misery was the true name of tears.
