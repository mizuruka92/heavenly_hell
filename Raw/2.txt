It was a fine day, where warmth and lush lands were the norm.
Asgard stood upon a great sphere that circled the sun, a world girded by a shining ring.

To the naked eye, the sphere would appear as a gaseous giant, yet within it the Creator had woven His magic — shaping realms unseen by mortals, the final destination men would call heaven.
There, gods and goddesses dwelled in their own dominions, and Asgard was one of them.

I was born in another sphere — the largest of all — made of the same gases and crowned with magical lands where giants resided, until Lord Odin invited my brothers and me to live in Asgard.
Or rather, to be held captive, for we had no freedom.

My eldest brother, with his mighty jaws, was bound deep within Asgard.
Though we were close, I could never visit him.
My second brother was cast into the seas of Midgard, his body growing so vast that only the endless ocean could contain it.
As for me — I slept in a guest chamber. No net ensnared me, yet my hideous appearance became known, and people shunned me.
So I chose to hide.

From afar, I could hear the Einherjars training.
They fought daily. They died daily.
And each morning, they rose anew.
Those who survived would spend the night in splendor — feasting on the finest food in all of Asgard, served by none other than Lady Freya, the first Valkyrie.
Those who fell would awaken that very evening, but skipping the feast to their dishonor, slept through the night ready to die again when the morrow comes.

Among them, Johann was the strongest, the fiercest — Lady Freya’s favorite.
She regarded him almost like a son.
Yet for all his might, kindness was not among his virtues.
Some said he had traded compassion for power.
Arrogant, rude, and overconfident would be an understatement of his deeds.
And on that day, he slew countless of his fellow Einherjar — all for the pleasure of feasting that night.
